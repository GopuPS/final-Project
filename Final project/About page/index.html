HISTORY

In 1946, Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo K.K. launched Sony. (Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation, The predecessor of Sony Group Corporation).

The company's initial capital was barely 190,000 yen, and it only had 20 workers. Masaru Ibuka was the company's original creator, and his mission

statement read: "Establish an ideal factory that stresses a spirit of freedom and open mindedness that will, through technology, contribute to Japanese

culture." Sony has continuing to put out a tonne of innovative products By adhering to the company's core goal to "do what has never been done before," it has

produced "Japan's first" and "world's first" items.

THE FAULT IN THE START

The Tokyo Telecommunications Research Institute, later known as Sony, released the rice cooker as its first product.

For Ibuka, a co-founder of Sony, and his team, it was a remarkable first failure. It was uncommon to get delicious rice

since the rice cooker often produced undercooked or overdone rice. World War II had barely finished at the time of this.
